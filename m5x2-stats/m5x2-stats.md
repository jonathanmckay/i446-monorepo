## Observations & Flags

### Structural / Categorization Issues

- **Principal Paydown** — Not an operating expense; it's balance sheet activity (debt reduction / equity building). Including it in cash-out inflates spend and obscures true operating performance. Most multifamily operators separate debt service from operating expenses for NOI analysis.

- **Capex Turn ($48K in r888) vs Turns ($8K in r203)** — Turn costs appear in two places. Needs confirmation whether these are truly different (e.g., r203 = make-ready labor, r888 = capital improvements during turns) or potential double-counting.

- **m5x2 m (Travel, corporate labor)** — Looks like asset management / corporate overhead, not a property-level expense. Mixing it with property ops makes it harder to benchmark against industry comps. Most operators track this above the property line.

### Numbers Worth a Second Look

- **Interest at 37.9%** of total cash-out is high, suggesting significant leverage. Stabilized multifamily typically runs debt service (P&I) closer to 30–35% of gross revenue.

- **Property Tax at -$186 in Jan 2026** — Likely a small refund/adjustment. 2025 had $668K in property tax (5.8% of spend). If Jan is truly near-zero, expect a big catch-up later in the year that will skew monthly comparisons.

- **Capex Discretionary at $111K in January** — Already 12.4% of full 2025 discretionary capex ($894K). Worth confirming this isn't a timing/accrual issue.

### General Note

The coded category names (r202, m5x2, r888, h5c7) are not standard multifamily chart-of-account labels. Mapping to industry-standard categories (NARPM, NAA, or lender reporting format) would improve benchmarking and investor readability.
